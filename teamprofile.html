July

2023

Women's Way was founded by Jiya Jaiswal and Riya Sharma, the first two members and current Presidents of the club. Their original purpose for the newly created club was to be a public non-profit organization available to the public in North Texas. With a new Instagram page opened up with promotional content and humble beginnings, this was just the start.

August

2023



Later on, the organization decided to expand their campaign at their local school, Rock Hill High School, a part of the Prosper Independent School District. Additionally, Soham Panda, the new Technical Lead and only male officer also joined the organization to contribute to the goal of Women's Way and work on an array of technicalities.

September

2023

The presidents, Jiya and Riya, decided on finalizing the tasks and contributions of their respective positions in the organization as we slowly started to get more serious and engage with the school. Our first sponsor was Aly Jordan, a mathematics teacher here at Rock Hill.

October

2023

We held our first ever officer elections for the club. These new positions that any high school student regardless of class could have ran for a vote and gave a speech to earn the position were held formally. From the results, Nehmat Kaur was our Historian, Hadeeqa Rizvi being our Social Media Manager, and Reese Apita being our Secretary. At this time of date, all six officers are sophomores at Rock Hill.

January

2024



As 2024 began, our meetings during school times have situated in a much bigger space, the LGI (Large Group Instruction) Room. We also earned a new sponsor at that time, Kylie Fainstein (Fain), an english teacher here at Rock Hill. Additionally, our club was official at the school and was qualified for a spot in the 2023-24 RHHS Yearbook Select.

March

2024



In March, we decided to bring in a guest speaker to expand and discuss ideas and important things that centralize with women. Mrs. Nickens, a former combat veteran and official governmental seater in Collin County came in to talk at our March meeting.

May

2024



As we closed up our first and official year of Women's Way, we concluded our monthly meetings with fun activities such as making slime balls for coping with stress over AP Exams and playing informational and educational games to win prizes over a variety of topics concerning women empowerment, discussions, women in technology, and much more.

August

2024



After our very succesful first year, we prepped for the second year of Women's Way. We introduced ourselves at the Annual Fish Camp where we recruited new members and explained our goals as a club. Our 2023-24 officers attended only.



September

2024

We held our interest meeting for the 24-25 year, and so many members had joined! We also discussed on different things such as new officer positions, our plans for the rest of 2024, networking, and any events that newly recruited members could be involved in.

October

2024

In our October meeting, we held many spooky events such as the Trunk Or Treat in the back parking lot of the school. Our second officer elections for the club were also concluded. From the results, Ananya Jha was our Historian, Ayana Tyagi being our Secretary, and Hadeeqa Rizvi being our Social Media Manager once again! At this time of date, all six officers are juniors at Rock Hill.

November

2024

In our November meeting, we made bracelets with beads and discussed important women in history. Additionally, we covered more topics over common issues with feminism and etcera. We concluded with a prize for the top 3 best bead bracelets among the members!

December

2024



In the last meeting of the year, Women's Way collaborated with Aid For Need and had a joint meeting for the December series. We conducted OOTDs of both clubs' members, held a potluck of snacks and drinks, discussed important events of donations and women in society, and we held our biggest period product drive with A4N with over 50 hygiene products donated.

With that, we concluded a great year. Thank you to everyone who had supported us throughout the year. It would have not been done without you all!
